üìÑ Executive Summary

This proposal establishes two complementary bridge programs at CAMP Community College (CAMP) within the Virginia Community College System (VCCS): a Paramedic-to-Registered Nurse (Paramedic‚ÜíRN) bridge and a Registered Nurse-to-Paramedic (RN‚ÜíParamedic) bridge. Both programs leverage existing licensure and clinical competencies to reduce time-to-completion while meeting all governing regulatory requirements.


The Paramedic‚ÜíRN pathway awards credit and clinical equivalency for documented paramedic competencies, enabling NRP-certified paramedics to complete an Associate Degree in Nursing (ADN) in approximately 4 semesters rather than the traditional 5‚Äì6. The RN‚ÜíParamedic pathway similarly awards equivalency for documented nursing clinical hours, enabling licensed RNs to achieve National Registry Paramedic (NRP) certification in approximately 3 semesters.


Both pathways comply with the Virginia Board of Nursing (18 VAC 90-27), the Virginia Office of EMS (12 VAC 5-31), CoAEMSP Standards, and ACEN Accreditation Standards for Associate Degree Nursing programs within VCCS Policy.

üéØ Program Rationale

Workforce need: The Hampton Roads region and surrounding Tidewater area face critical shortages in both hospital nursing and prehospital EMS. Cross-trained professionals improve surge capacity, reduce lateral hiring costs, and increase career mobility for existing EMS and nursing staff at partner agencies including Norfolk Fire & Rescue and Suffolk Fire & Rescue.


Equity of competency: Paramedics routinely perform advanced clinical skills ‚Äî IV/IO access, endotracheal intubation, pharmacological interventions, 12-lead ECG interpretation, and critical care transport ‚Äî that are equivalent to or exceed many clinical requirements in associate-degree nursing programs. A structured bridge allows formal recognition of these competencies against nursing direct client care (DCC) hours without compromising patient safety or BON standards.


Institutional alignment: CAMP's existing CoAEMSP-accredited paramedic program provides the infrastructure, clinical partnerships, and faculty necessary to launch both bridges with minimal new resource investment. Program integration with VCCS Career Studies Certificates and existing ADN pathways ensures seamless transcript articulation statewide.

Regulatory Framework

All applicable governing bodies, code citations, and compliance requirements for both bridge programs.

‚ÑπÔ∏èBoth programs must simultaneously satisfy nursing education regulations (BON), EMS education regulations (OEMS), and national accreditation standards. Where requirements overlap, the more stringent standard governs.

‚öñÔ∏è Virginia Board of Nursing (BON) 18 VAC 90-27

18 VAC 90-27-30 ‚Äî Nursing Education Program Requirements

Approved nursing programs must have a clearly defined mission/philosophy, curriculum organized around NCLEX competencies, qualified nursing faculty (MSN minimum; DNP preferred for ADN programs), and adequate clinical placement agreements. Programs must submit for BON approval prior to enrolling students.

18 VAC 90-27-100 ‚Äî Clinical Hours & Direct Client Care (DCC)

For ADN programs: minimum 50% of total curriculum hours in clinical laboratory and clinical settings. Simulation may substitute for up to 50% of clinical hours with BON approval. Paramedic clinical experience credited under this bridge counts as Direct Client Care hours when validated by program faculty through a structured credit-for-prior-learning (CPL) portfolio and skills demonstration.

18 VAC 90-27-120 ‚Äî Faculty Requirements

Nursing faculty must hold RN licensure in Virginia plus a graduate degree in nursing. Faculty supervising clinical must maintain current clinical competency. EMS subject matter experts may serve as adjunct instructors but cannot serve as the faculty-of-record for nursing clinical courses without nursing credentials.

18 VAC 90-19-240 ‚Äî Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) in Nursing

Virginia BON permits programs to accept validated prior learning credit, including documented clinical hours from other licensed healthcare programs, provided the program has written policies, evaluation criteria, and faculty oversight. Paramedic SMC documentation constitutes qualifying prior learning evidence for DCC credit.

üöë Virginia Office of EMS (OEMS) 12 VAC 5-31

12 VAC 5-31-300 ‚Äî Paramedic Education Program Approval

Paramedic programs must be approved by OEMS and maintain CoAEMSP accreditation. Programs must submit curriculum to OEMS for review, maintain clinical affiliate agreements, and ensure all clinical hours meet the National EMS Education Standards (NEMSES) and CoAEMSP curriculum framework.

12 VAC 5-31-310 ‚Äî Clinical Education Requirements

Minimum hours per NEMSES/CoAEMSP: 490+ hours of combined hospital clinical (minimum 160 hrs) and field internship (minimum 330 hrs). RN clinical experience credited toward paramedic program must be mapped to specific EMS clinical objectives and validated by EMS medical director. An RN's documented hospital clinical hours (DCC) may satisfy up to 160 of the hospital clinical hours through CPL.

12 VAC 5-31-330 ‚Äî Medical Director Oversight

A licensed Virginia physician must serve as Medical Director for the paramedic program and must review and approve all clinical competency equivalencies for the RN‚ÜíParamedic bridge. Medical Director must sign off on any CPL granting clinical hour equivalency.

üèÖ CoAEMSP Standards 2024 Edition

Standard II ‚Äî Program Effectiveness

Annual outcomes must meet or exceed thresholds: NREMT first-attempt pass rate ‚â•70%, completion rate ‚â•70%, positive placement ‚â•70%. Bridge programs must track and report outcomes separately and ensure CPL students meet all NRP psychomotor and cognitive competencies.

Standard IV ‚Äî Curriculum

The paramedic curriculum must align with NEMSES. RN bridge students who receive CPL must still demonstrate each core paramedic psychomotor skill via lab or field performance. CPL reduces instructional hours but does NOT eliminate competency verification.

Standard V ‚Äî Resources & Faculty

Paramedic program faculty must hold current NRP certification and appropriate credentials. Joint nursing/EMS courses must clearly delineate who serves as faculty-of-record for each academic component. All clinical affiliates must have current affiliation agreements with CAMP.

üéì ACEN Accreditation Standards Standard 4 ‚Äî Curriculum

ACEN Standard 4.6 ‚Äî Curriculum Design

The ADN curriculum must be organized to ensure progressive learning. Bridge courses must articulate within the existing CAMP ADN sequence. CPL credits must be documented in the student's academic plan and transcript with clear notation of how they were earned.

ACEN Standard 4.8 ‚Äî Learning Resources

Students must have access to appropriate learning resources including simulation labs, library access, and clinical placements sufficient for their bridge program coursework. Paramedic bridge students must access nursing simulation lab resources.

üèõ VCCS Policy Compliance

VCCS Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) Policy ‚Äî 5.4.2

The Virginia Community College System permits up to 25% of credits required for a degree to be awarded through CPL mechanisms including portfolio assessment, challenge exams, and validated prior learning from licensure programs. Paramedic‚ÜíRN and RN‚ÜíParamedic bridges both operate within this ceiling.

VCCS Curriculum Approval ‚Äî SCHEV Notification

New academic programs at Virginia community colleges require SCHEV notification. Bridge programs structured as curriculum modifications to existing approved ADN and Paramedic programs may qualify for expedited review rather than full new program approval, depending on credit hours added/modified. Legal counsel and SCHEV consultation recommended.

SMC ‚Üí Nursing Direct Client Care Hours Conversion

Explicit mapping of each Paramedic Skills Minimum Competency (SMC) to equivalent nursing Direct Client Care (DCC) hours per BON and ACEN standards.

‚ö†Ô∏èPer BON 18 VAC 90-27, all CPL clinical equivalency must be (1) documented in writing, (2) validated by faculty review, (3) approved by the ADN program director, and (4) based on skills that map directly to a specific nursing clinical objective. The conversion table below serves as the foundational CPL documentation.

üîë Conversion Methodology

Each paramedic SMC is evaluated across three dimensions: skill complexity, patient population applicability, and BON clinical objective alignment. Hours equivalency is calculated based on the average time a nursing student would spend achieving the same competency in a supervised clinical setting, divided by the number of validated paramedic exposures documented in call logs or SMC records.

Partial

Elective
Credit-only ‚Äî paramedic skill noted but nursing hours credited at no more than 25%
